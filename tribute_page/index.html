Dr Viktor Frankl

The man who survived a concentration Camp

Here's a Timeline of Viktor Frankl's life:

  • 1905 - Born in Vienna, Austria.
  • 1923 - Graduated from High School.
  • 1924 - Frank's first Scientific paper was published in the international journal of Psychoanalysis.
  • 1929 - Organized youth Counselling Centers to adress the high number of teens suicides.
  • 1930 - Frank earned his M.D, frank also was responsible for the treatment of suicidal woman.
  • 1937 - Began a private practice of psychoanalysis.
  • 1938 - Nazi annexiation of Austria and limited his opportunity to treat patients.
  • 1940 - Joined the Rothschild Hospital, the only hospital in Vienna still admitting Jews.
  • 1942 - Frankl and his family were sent to a concentration camp, where his father died.
  • 1944 - Frankl and the surviving members of his famil were transported to Auschwitz, Frankl spent 3 years in 4 concentration camps
  • 1945 - 1970 - Following the war, Frankl became head of neurology department of the Viena Polyclinic Hospital, and established a private practice in his home, he worked with patients until his retirement in 1970
